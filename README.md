# Employee Compensation & Salary Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing employee compensation and workforce salary trends using Microsoft Excel. The dashboard provides insights into payroll distribution, salary structures, workforce demographics, and compensation patterns across departments, cities, education levels, gender, and experience groups.

The objective of the project is to help HR professionals and management monitor compensation metrics and make data-driven workforce planning decisions.

---

## Business Problem

Organizations need visibility into payroll expenses, salary distribution, and workforce compensation patterns to support budgeting and compensation planning.

This dashboard answers key HR questions such as:

* Which department has the highest payroll cost?
* Which city offers the highest average salary?
* How does experience impact compensation?
* Are there salary differences across education levels and gender?
* What are the organization's key compensation metrics?

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains information for 50 employees, including:

* Employee ID
* Department
* Experience
* Education Level
* Gender
* City
* Monthly Salary

## Key Insights

* Marketing recorded the highest payroll expenditure.
* Bangalore employees received the highest average compensation.
* Employees with Master's degrees earned the highest salaries.
* Female employees earned higher average salaries than male employees in the dataset.
* Salary generally increased with employee experience.
